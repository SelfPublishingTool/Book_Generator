## The Prep-Once System

### How Much Protein Do You Actually Need

The number you hear most often is 0.7 to 1 gram of protein per pound of body weight per day. For a 160-pound person, that means roughly 112 to 160 grams of protein daily. For weight loss specifically, staying toward the higher end of that range — around 1 gram per pound — helps preserve the muscle you have while your body burns fat. More muscle means a faster metabolism, which means the weight you lose stays off.

A simpler way to think about it: aim for 25 to 40 grams of protein per meal across three meals a day, plus one or two protein-rich snacks. Every recipe in this book is built to hit that target.

You do not need to count every gram. Follow the plan, use the recipes, and the math takes care of itself.

### The 6 Protein Sources in This Book

Chicken — lean, versatile, and affordable. Chicken breast and thighs anchor dozens of recipes in this book. Thighs add more flavor and hold up better in meal prep without drying out.

Beef — ground beef, sirloin, and stew meat bring iron, zinc, and serious staying power. Used in bowls, stews, and chili throughout.

Fish — salmon, cod, tuna, and shrimp deliver omega-3 fatty acids alongside high-quality protein. Salmon in particular is one of the best weight-loss proteins because of its effect on hunger hormones.

Eggs — the most complete protein source in any kitchen. Whole eggs, egg whites, and hard-boiled eggs appear throughout the breakfast and snack chapters.

Legumes — black beans, white beans, chickpeas, and lentils bring plant-based protein plus fiber, which extends satiety even further. Often paired with animal proteins to boost the total protein count while adding texture.

Dairy — Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, and reduced-fat cheese are protein powerhouses hiding in plain sight. Greek yogurt and cottage cheese in particular are used extensively in this book for both savory and sweet recipes.

No single source exceeds 30 percent of the total recipes in this book. That is the variety guarantee.

### The Simple Plate Formula

Every meal in this book follows the same invisible structure: ½ protein + ¼ smart carb + ¼ vegetables, with a sauce or seasoning that ties it together. You do not need to measure your plate. The recipes are already built this way. Once you see it in the recipes, you will start to see it everywhere.

### The Sunday Method

The entire week of food comes together in one prep session. Here is the sequence that makes it efficient:

Step 1 — Proteins first (45–60 minutes). Start with whatever takes the longest. Sheet pan proteins go in the oven. Ground meat goes on the stove. Eggs go in a pot of boiling water. Salmon goes in the oven after the chicken comes out. Everything that requires heat gets started before you touch anything else.

Step 2 — Grains and legumes while proteins cook (15–20 minutes). Rice cooker on. Quinoa on the stove. Canned beans drained and rinsed. These are hands-off once they are started.

Step 3 — Vegetables and assembly (20–25 minutes). Roast or chop vegetables. Mix sauces. Portion everything into containers.

Total active time: under 2 hours. Total hands-on effort: less than 90 minutes.

### Fridge vs. Freezer Storage Rules

Fridge (up to 4 days): cooked chicken, ground beef, fish, egg-based dishes, grain bowls, soups, stews, cooked vegetables.

Freezer (up to 3 months): chili, soups, stews, meatballs, cooked ground beef, most casseroles. Do not freeze: egg dishes, cooked fish (texture suffers), salads, anything with avocado.

Label every container with the contents and the date. Use glass containers when possible — they reheat evenly and do not absorb odors.

### Recipe 1 — High-Protein Egg & Veggie Muffins

Protein Source: ð¥ Eggs | Yield: 12 muffins (3 per serving) | Prep: 10 min | Cook: 22 min

Ingredients:

8 large eggs

½ cup cottage cheese (2%)

1 cup fresh spinach, chopped

½ cup red bell pepper, diced

½ cup mushrooms, diced

¼ cup shredded cheddar cheese

½ tsp garlic powder

Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions:

Preheat oven to 375°F. Grease a 12-cup muffin tin well with cooking spray.

In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs and cottage cheese until smooth.

Stir in the spinach, bell pepper, mushrooms, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.

Pour the mixture evenly into the prepared muffin cups, filling each about ¾ full.

Top each cup with a small pinch of shredded cheddar.

Bake for 20–22 minutes until the egg muffins are set in the center and lightly golden on top.

Let cool for 5 minutes before removing. Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days.

Nutrition Facts (per serving — 3 muffins):

Calories: 285 | Protein: 26g | Carbs: 5g | Fat: 18g

### Recipe 4 — Turkey Sausage & Sweet Potato Hash

Protein Source: ð Chicken/Poultry | Yield: 4 servings | Prep: 10 min | Cook: 25 min

1 red bell pepper, diced

Instructions:

## BONUS 3 — High-Protein Eating Out Guide

Staying on plan when life happens.

The plan works best at home. But life means restaurants, work lunches, and road trips. Here is how to stay on track at the 10 most common chains in the US.

McDonald's

✅ Order: Egg McMuffin + side of scrambled eggs (36g protein together) | Grilled Chicken Sandwich, no sauce

❌ Avoid: McFlurry, French Fries as a main side, Crispy Chicken (fried)

Protein hack: Order two Egg McMuffins for a quick 28g breakfast under $5.

Chipotle

✅ Order: Burrito bowl with double chicken or steak, black beans, fajita veggies, fresh salsa, Greek yogurt instead of sour cream (40g+ protein)

❌ Avoid: Chips and guacamole as your primary calories, cheese overload

Protein hack: Double meat always. The extra cost is worth it. Get the bowl, not the burrito — saves 300 calories.

Panera Bread

✅ Order: You Pick 2 — Ten Vegetable Soup + Chicken Noodle Soup | Fuji Apple Chicken Salad (full size)

❌ Avoid: Mac & Cheese bowls, pastries, lemonade

Protein hack: Ask for an extra side of chicken on any salad.

Subway

✅ Order: Oven-Roasted Chicken or Turkey Breast on whole wheat, load vegetables, no mayo (30g+ protein in a footlong)

❌ Avoid: Meatball marinara, footlong cookies, creamy sauces

Protein hack: Double the protein. It costs $1–2 extra and nearly doubles your protein count.

Chick-fil-A

✅ Order: Grilled Chicken Sandwich (no sauce) + Grilled Nuggets 8pc | Grilled Market Salad

❌ Avoid: Waffle fries as your only side, Chick-fil-A Sauce (280 calories), milkshakes

Protein hack: Grilled Nuggets (25g protein, 130 calories) are one of the best fast food protein options in the country.

Olive Garden

✅ Order: Herb-Grilled Salmon | Chicken Margherita | Soup and salad as lighter option

❌ Avoid: Unlimited breadsticks (they will derail you before your meal arrives), Fettuccine Alfredo

Protein hack: Ask for the grilled chicken upgrade on any pasta to add 20g protein while keeping the dish satisfying.

Applebee's

✅ Order: Cedar Salmon | 7 oz House Sirloin with steamed vegetables | Pepper-Crusted Sirloin & Shrimp

❌ Avoid: Full-size loaded appetizers as your meal, anything described as "crispy" or "loaded"

Starbucks

The Cheesecake Factory

✅ Order: SkinnyLicious Grilled Salmon | SkinnyLicious Chicken Soft Tacos | Grilled Turkey Burger on lettuce wrap

Protein hack: The SkinnyLicious menu is legitimate — use it every time.

Domino's
